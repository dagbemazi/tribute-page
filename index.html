Dr. Joseph George Williams, QSO QSM


Here is a timeline of Dr. Joseph Williams' life:

  • 1934 - Born in Aitutaki, Cook Islands.

  • 1960 - Graduated from Otago Medical School and later completed a Masters in Public Healthatthe University of Hawaii.

  • 1964 - Returned to Cook Islands, where he worked as Medical Superintendent while researching tropical diseases, including filariasis (elephantiasis) .

  • 1968 - He was elected to the Cook Islands Parliament for the electorate of Aitutaki.

  • 1974 - Awarded the Queen's Service Medal QSM in New Zealand. Served as Minister of Health and Education through to 1978.

  • 1995 - Served as a member of the World Health Organization's executive board.

  • 1998 - Served as a member of the Program Review Groupt for the Global Elimination of Lymphatic Filariasis.

  • 2004 - Awarded the Pasifika Medical Association Service Award.

  • 2011 - Appointed a Companion of the Queen's Service Order, for services to the Cook Islands commution in the New Zealand New Year Honours.

  • 2015 - Named inaugural Patron of the Pasifika Medical Association

  • 2016 - Received the World Health Organisation's Award of Appreciation for his contribution to eliminating lymphatic filariasis.

  • 2020 - Tested positive for COVID-19 on 13th August, died on 4th September aged 85.
